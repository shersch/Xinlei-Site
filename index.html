Welcome

Xinlei Chu is an up-and-coming composer originating from northern China. She began studying music at the age of 7 by taking violin lessons. When she was 13 years old, she decided to expand her musical horizons by studying voice and piano. While attending high school, Xinlei started studying composition under the guidance of music professors from the local university, which prepared her for her acceptance and eventual attendance of the Tianjin Conservatory of Music. Upon the completion of her bachelor's degree in music composition, she moved to Beijing, where she was immediately hired to work as a composer and producer for Yi-Ling-She record company. During her tenure there, Xinlei wrote and produced pop songs alonside many popular Chinese artists, as well as compose and produce music for various Chinese television shows. In 2008, she was hired at Beijing Kylin Network of Information Science and Technology as a music composer, producer, and sound designer for video games. Throughout her employment at Kylin, Xinlei assisted in the production and completion of the music and sound for over a dozen video games. In 2012, Xinlei came to the United States to further her education by earning a master of music degree in composition at Duquesne University's Mary Pappert School of Music. While studying under Professor Lynn Emberg Purse, Xinlei was able to focus on 20th century music, as well as electronic composition. Currently, Xinlei resides in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, where she continues her education privately with her mentor, Professor David Stock.

On Horseback

While visiting her hometown for a brief time, Xinlei recorded Mongolian Throat Singing and Mongolian Long Song. She combined technology and traditional folk music to write an electronic piece based on the vocal material she recorded. The names of the four movements are four elements that Xinlei chose to represent the lifestyle of Inner Mongolia in China.
